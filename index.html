My Story
Once upon a time...
From a young age I was interested in computers and Technologies. As I grew up, I started playing video games and back in my head I always wondered how is it that I press the 'W' key and my player in the monitor starts moving forward. I really found all these technologies very fascinating and overwhelming. Well some of my questions got answered by my mentor, some by my teachers and mostly by the power of Interent. Check out my full story in my blog "HOW I BECAME A PROGRAMMER".
1998
My BirthYear. From a very young age, I was very interested in computer and technologies.
2014
I got introduced to my first programming language that was JAVA. I got interested more in programming and the things I can do with it. I had a good mentor and teacher who made my base very strong and thus I have strong fundamental knowledge in programming concepts.
2016
Got into college. It was here that I got involved more into programming and came to know why technology is so important in every industry. Here I was introduced with web technologies HTML, CSS and concepts like RDBMS with languages like MySql.
2018
Started learning PYTHON, my very first programming language which I learned by myself. This cleared most of my programming concepts and algorithms. Also this was the time I figured out I was gonna be a Programmer.
2019
Started using libraries like Tkinter and learnt some big frameworks such as Django and Kivy. Also this was the time when I started making projects and learned about version control system.
2020
Turning point in my life, started my journey to become a Web Developer. Created a scalable backend project with Django (Triburg Web). Started learning other technologies related to web like HTML, CSS and switched from Python to Javascript as my primary language.
2021
Financial Independance. Also I want to learn the MERN stack and work on some awesome projects with it.

HOW I BECAME A PROGRAMMER
LETS START FROM THE BEGINNING As a kid I was always curious and very interested to solve problems in a different way and approach them in a way that makes it easier to understand and deal with. While credits to my mom for this. As I grew up, I developed a keen interest in computers and ofcourse that was because I loved playing video games. But thats when I got this urge to know how these things are actually made. BUILDING A BASE During schooling, I always opted for subjects that were more related to computers and technology. I was introduced with HTML and basic computer knowledge in my high school (8th -10th). Later in my secondary and senior secondary (11th & 12th), I opted for Informatics Practices in which I was introduced with my very first programming language, which was JAVA. Although it was not very thorough with the language but I really made a strong base with the programming concepts and algorithms. With that knowledge I got interested to learn more, but didnt get the opportunity to study CSE in any of the prestigious colleges here in India be it IIT, NIT, BITs etc. So I left that and got into something that at that time I thought was best for me that was B.F.Tech form NIFT. WHY NIFT ? If you dont know what B.F.Tech is or what NIFT is, I will summarise it for you. Basically, its a managerial degree combined with learning technical knowledge and enhancing soft skills to have a more practical approach in the real world scenarios generally focused in Design and Fashion industry but the approach can be applied in any situation of your life. I learnt a lot from this phase of my life. It made me a more confident person and gave me the tools to deal with any problem effiently and effectively. WHAT I GOT FROM COLLEGE LIFE ? Now I was not just learning life lessons form my college, infact I got interested into web design and development here in college only. I got introduced with design principles, color wheel, golden ration, geometrical concepts, UI/UX and most importantly how and when to apply which knowledge and skill to crack a problem or design a creative solution. And here is when I got introduced with languages like languages like CSS and Python. Then I started learning more and more about Python in particular by my own as the sessions from my college were not enough for me. FOUND A PROGRAMMER IN ME Python became the first programming language which I learned properly. I found about online learning platforms like Udemy and Coursera during this time and I invested more than 1 years to fully understand the language along with deeper programming concepts and more complex and real problems. The next year I got fully engaged in making projects using python and as a proud learner showing off them to my friends and family. Well, least could I have known that no one was interested in some toy projects and how a product's mechanism or code works and how big of an achievements they really were to me. Little disheartened but not all hopes lost. INTERNET IS A BOON Well internet has always been a boon to me, sometimes it guides me and sometimes it simply motivates me in ways no one could ever imagine. So one fine evening I got recommended this video titled "Don't be a programmer" (source: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=EiKK04Ht8QI) and I didnt knew how youtube knew how I was feeling or so I thought, and then I watched the video and it completely changed my perspective towards programming. I knew where I would fit in this puzzle of a world, I knew exactly what I had to do to get to become what I aspire for. BECOMMING A PROGRAMMER A quest for becoming the best of me began. With new found meaning of who I want to be, I really got serious about it and the journey began. Well it was a hard journey from the start. As I was learning all by my own with no one to help me if I was stuck in my code, I would have to decode my code manually. Sometimes I just called it off and let it go and sometimes I stuck to it for days, weeks even months to solve a problem. Sometimes the bugs were silly and sometimes they were unimaginably difficult to even find, solving them used to be like a dream. Talking about dreams, yes I have found solution to my bugs in code in my dreams as I used to be so engrossed in them. Well then came my old friend-cum-mentor-cum-guide "THE INTERNET". So I learned how to Google things and read answers form StackOverflow pretty early in my journey to becomming a programmer. These are pretty normal and basic things that happens with every programmer but in my case I learned them in a difficult way. REAL PROGRAMMING When I became pretty comfortable with programming and got some confidence to build anything that I could imagine. I took up some very serious projects and took them as my internship projects. In that way I would be working with a deadline to finish the project and finish them in time. The only problem with this idea was that I never anticipated the fact that coding a project is just the first phase in building a project, the second is to present it to people who dont understand programming and really have no interest in your ideas and how it was made and why is it so difficult to bringing an idea to life. My grades fell drastically. THE FINAL TEST Regardless of my setback, I refused to give up on my project and extended my project in the next internship. For my graduation project, I opted for a company that could have potential flaws in their working methodologies and so that I could come up with a solution to solve their problems. And learning from my mistakes, I decided to build a solution that could be useful, user friendly and easy to understand & present at the same time. Well here is where I built the "Triburg Web", one of my most complicated and difficult project not only from coding perspective but from learning and presentation perspective as well. You can know more about the learnings and project details in the "My Works" section. WHAT AM I NOW? During lockdown, I engaged myself into learning something that I wanted to do for a very long time, "Web Development". So I started learning web technologies and especially JavaScript. Well I must say I found Javascript to be equally fun and interesting to learn as was with Python. I picked up the language pretty fast as I was clear with the programming concept and basics. But I wanted to mster JavaScript, so I preffered a Udemy course for this, The Complete JavaScript Course 2021: From Zero to Expert! by Jonas Schmedtmann (https://www.udemy.com/course/the-complete-javascript-course/). I was almost complete with the course by October and only the final project was left but then the course was updated with latest and new concepts of the language. At first I was very disappointed, as I didn't wanted to restart the course from the beginning but I stayed true to my goal becoming the master of the language, so I decided to restart the course from the beginning and learn all the new things as much as I could. After 3 months, I am glad to say that I restarted the course and it really helped my coding style and all my base concepts about the language are more than clear. Now I can say I have a pretty strong grasp over the language but still got a lot more to learn. WHAT'S NEXT ? As soon as I will finish the course I will start learning MERN stack. As I said earlier, I dont want to build toy apps, I believe learning can only be at its peak when you pick up something that is almost impossible for you to achieve with the knowledge you have and only then you will find ways to approach and solve the problem that are really out of the box, innovative and creative.

So I learned how to Google things and read answers form StackOverflow pretty early in my journey to becomming a programmer. These are pretty normal and basic things that happens with every programmer but in my case I learned them in a difficult way. REAL PROGRAMMING When I became pretty comfortable with programming and got some confidence to build anything that I could imagine. I took up some very serious projects and took them as my internship projects. In that way I would be working with a deadline to finish the project and finish them in time. The only problem with this idea was that I never anticipated the fact that coding a project is just the first phase in building a project, the second is to present it to people who dont understand programming and really have no interest in your ideas and how it was made and why is it so difficult to bringing an idea to life. My grades fell drastically. THE FINAL TEST Regardless of my setback, I refused to give up on my project and extended my project in the next internship. For my graduation project, I opted for a company that could have potential flaws in their working methodologies and so that I could come up with a solution to solve their problems. And learning from my mistakes, I decided to build a solution that could be useful, user friendly and easy to understand & present at the same time. Well here is where I built the "Triburg Web", one of my most complicated and difficult project not only from coding perspective but from learning and presentation perspective as well. You can know more about the learnings and project details in the "My Works" section. WHAT AM I NOW? During lockdown, I engaged myself into learning something that I wanted to do for a very long time, "Web Development". So I started learning web technologies and especially JavaScript. Well I must say I found Javascript to be equally fun and interesting to learn as was with Python. I picked up the language pretty fast as I was clear with the programming concept and basics. But I wanted to mster JavaScript, so I preffered a Udemy course for this, The Complete JavaScript Course 2021: From Zero to Expert! by Jonas Schmedtmann (https://www.udemy.com/course/the-complete-javascript-course/). I was almost complete with the course by October and only the final project was left but then the course was updated with latest and new concepts of the language. At first I was very disappointed, as I didn't wanted to restart the course from the beginning but I stayed true to my goal becoming the master of the language, so I decided to restart the course from the beginning and learn all the new things as much as I could. After 3 months, I am glad to say that I restarted the course and it really helped my coding style and all my base concepts about the language are more than clear. Now I can say I have a pretty strong grasp over the language but still got a lot more to learn. WHAT'S NEXT ? As soon as I will finish the course I will start learning MERN stack. As I said earlier, I dont want to build toy apps, I believe learning can only be at its peak when you pick up something that is almost impossible for you to achieve with the knowledge you have and only then you will find ways to approach and solve the problem that are really out of the box, innovative and creative.
